[id: sl_007]
category: layout_considerations
topic: Lighting for vehicle display
answer: Vehicle paint and finish quality is highly sensitive to lighting angle and color temperature. Recommended color temperature for display vehicles is 4000K to 4500K (neutral white). Avoid warm lighting (below 3000K) which distorts metallic finishes. Each vehicle should have a minimum of four dedicated adjustable spots. For matte finishes, reduce specular highlights by using diffused lighting sources.
tags: lighting, display, paint, color-temperature

[id: sl_008]
category: layout_considerations
topic: Flooring load ratings for vehicle display
answer: A typical passenger electric vehicle weighs between 1,800 kg and 2,400 kg. Display platforms must be rated for at least 3x the vehicle weight to accommodate jacks and movement equipment. Standard commercial flooring is rated at 500 kg/sqm — verify with your structural engineer for elevated platforms or mezzanine installations.
tags: flooring, load-rating, structural, safety

[id: sl_009]
category: layout_considerations
topic: Minimum aisle clearance between display vehicles
answer: For customer safety and ADA compliance, minimum aisle clearance between display vehicles is 1.5 meters (approximately 5 feet). For high-footfall premium showrooms, 2 meters is recommended to allow comfortable viewing from multiple angles without congestion.
tags: aisle, clearance, ADA, safety, spacing

[id: sl_010]
category: layout_considerations
topic: Electrical requirements for display vehicles
answer: Display vehicles should remain connected to a low-amperage trickle charger to maintain battery health and keep infotainment systems live for customer interaction. Each display bay should have a dedicated Level 1 or Level 2 charging outlet (120V or 240V). Plan wiring runs during the initial fit-out — retrofitting is costly and disruptive.
tags: electrical, charging, display, infrastructure
